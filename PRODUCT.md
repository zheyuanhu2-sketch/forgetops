# Product

## Register

product

## Users

ForgetOps serves DataHub hackathon judges, privacy operations leads, data governance engineers, and data platform owners. They need to understand a right-to-erasure case quickly, inspect why each asset is in scope, distinguish permitted mutations from policy exceptions, approve a bounded plan, and verify the result without trusting an opaque agent narrative.

## Product Purpose

ForgetOps turns DataHub context into an auditable privacy-operation control loop: discover the subject's data footprint, trace downstream impact, apply organization-defined policy, preview actions, require explicit approval, execute safely, verify every outcome, and return evidence to DataHub. Success means a reviewer can explain and replay the complete case in under three minutes while raw subject identifiers never appear in plans, logs, or evidence artifacts.

## Brand Personality

Forensic, composed, decisive. The interface should feel like a trusted operations instrument with editorial clarity: technically rigorous enough for an engineer, legible enough for a judge, and restrained enough that policy exceptions remain more important than visual effects.

## Anti-references

- Generic admin dashboards made from identical cards and decorative metrics.
- Neon hacker terminals, AI-purple gradients, glassmorphism, or cinematic effects that obscure the task.
- Chatbot-first layouts that hide evidence behind conversational claims.
- Data theater: unlabeled graphs, fake precision, success confetti, or green states that ignore legal holds.
- Dense enterprise tables that force judges to reconstruct the story themselves.

## Design Principles

1. **Evidence before action.** Every decision exposes the DataHub signal that caused it before an approval control appears.
2. **Exceptions are first-class outcomes.** Legal hold, retention, and manual review remain visible and prevent false closure.
3. **One case, one narrative.** Discovery, policy, execution, verification, and write-back read as a continuous trace rather than separate dashboards.
4. **Safety must be observable.** Dry-run state, approval state, plan identity, idempotency, and post-action checks are explicit interface objects.
5. **Demo clarity is product quality.** The primary case is understandable in under three minutes without narration carrying missing context.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. All controls must be keyboard accessible, focus-visible, and usable without color alone. Respect reduced-motion preferences, maintain 44-by-44-pixel touch targets where practical, use live regions for workflow state changes, and keep technical evidence readable at 200% zoom and on a 1280-by-720 laptop viewport.
